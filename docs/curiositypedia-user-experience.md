# CuriosityPedia: What It Feels Like to Use

CuriosityPedia is a research and learning product built around a simple idea: a good answer should not end your curiosity; it should give that curiosity somewhere meaningful to go next. Instead of behaving like a conventional search engine, where every query is an isolated transaction, or like a chatbot, where a long conversation eventually becomes an indistinct scroll of messages, CuriosityPedia turns exploration into a visible, lasting journey. You begin with one question, receive a researched answer supported by sources and real-world visual evidence, and then choose between two thoughtfully prepared directions. Every choice becomes a new branch. Over time, the product preserves not just what you learned, but the route your attention took through it.

The experience feels somewhere between opening an elegant reference book, following footnotes through an archive, and drawing a map of a fascinating conversation. Its visual language is intentionally editorial rather than futuristic: warm cream paper, near-black green ink, large serif headlines, clean sans-serif labels, fine rules, and flashes of coral, sky blue, and bright acid green. The page feels composed and tactile, with the seriousness of a well-designed magazine but enough color and motion to make discovery feel playful. It does not present curiosity as a blank productivity task. It presents it as an invitation.

## The first impression

The top of the product is a steady navigation bar. CuriosityPedia’s circular **C** mark sits on the left; the main destinations—**New drive**, **Library**, **Bookmarks**, **Usage**, and **Settings**—sit across the center; and the current identity appears on the right. A visitor can use the product as a guest, in which case the interface makes it clear that saved work belongs to that browser session, or sign in with a ChatGPT identity so journeys and preferences can follow the account. This account information is visible without dominating the experience.

The landing page is less like an empty search page and more like a curiosity desk already covered with promising leads. A ribbon of recommended “rabbit holes” moves across the page: specific questions drawn from current signals, the selected research personality, and—once the person has used CuriosityPedia—their own history. Each recommendation carries a small topic label, so a user can quickly scan ideas from science, history, design, cities, culture, technology, or the natural world. The ribbon can be refreshed with **Find new questions**, and clicking any question places it directly into the main question field.

At the center is a large, changing invitation such as “Ask a question. Open a world.” or “Follow your wonder.” Below it is a generous writing area rather than a cramped search box. The placeholder itself cycles through possible questions, helping someone who arrives interested but unsure where to begin. As the user types, CuriosityPedia can recognize a matching recommended question and offer the full wording. On a keyboard, the suggestion can be accepted with Tab; it can also be clicked. The user’s own text is never silently replaced. A visible character count makes the boundary clear, and the **Begin wonder** button remains unavailable until there is a meaningful question.

Two compact choices sit beneath the question. The first is the **Performer**: not a fictional character taking over the conversation, but a choice of explanatory temperament. **Sage** is patient, warm, and interested in deeper patterns. **Spark** looks for a useful surprise. **Mechanist** explains moving parts, causes, feedback, and failure modes. **Atlas** behaves like a fact-hunter, staying tightly anchored to documented people, places, organisms, events, objects, and phenomena. Selecting a performer subtly changes the kind of recommended questions shown and the voice in which the research will be presented. The second choice is the research model, giving the user a straightforward way to balance speed, depth, and cost without exposing a confusing wall of technical controls.

Around this practical center, CuriosityPedia keeps the mood alive. A small wall of quotations about curiosity and learning appears like colorful paper stickers, with links to their original sources. A rotating window of community artwork gives the page visual atmosphere, with clear artist credit and simple previous, pause, and next controls. Users who prefer less movement can disable motion in Settings.

## Watching a question become a researched turn

When the user presses **Begin wonder**, the product does not drop them into a generic spinner. The next page already has the shape of the answer that is coming. The chosen question becomes the main headline. The selected performer appears by name, and a live status area uses plain phrases such as **Buffering answer**, **Retrying**, or **Answer ready**. A structured skeleton previews the future composition: lines where the answer will appear, a space for visual material, evidence details, and two empty paths where the next questions will eventually arrive. This makes the wait feel like a page being assembled rather than an application being frozen.

The status language explains what is happening without pretending that the answer is instant. When research succeeds, the interface briefly confirms that the answer is ready and then places it into the completed stage. If something prevents the research from starting—such as a full saved-journey library or a rolling usage limit—the page says so directly and gives the relevant next action. If a research attempt fails, CuriosityPedia makes clear that an incomplete answer was not saved. The user can return safely instead of wondering whether a broken fragment has polluted the journey.

## The answer stage

The completed answer page is called the **Stage**, and that name captures its purpose: one question is given the room and hierarchy to be understood properly. At the top, a small label identifies the turn number and performer. The user’s question appears as the dominant headline, accompanied by a source count. The page avoids the visual rhythm of chat bubbles. The answer is treated as an authored research card.

Inside that card, the performer’s mark and name establish the chosen explanatory voice, while a note makes clear that the material came from live web research. The main answer is concise enough to read without losing the thread, but it contains numbered citation links exactly where claims depend on sources. Small tags identify the topic, the number of checked sources, and the fact that the work was researched live. A user can save this exact question to Bookmarks from the top of the card. A secondary menu provides a snapshot and a structured export for people who want a durable copy.

Visual evidence occupies its own deliberate area beside the prose on larger screens. This is not decoration presented as proof. The image is sourced factual material connected to the subject—a historical map, archival photograph, diagram, object, place, or other relevant record. A filmstrip lets the user move among available images, and the selected visual is paired with a caption and a short explanation of why it matters to the answer. When no responsible visual evidence is available, CuriosityPedia says so rather than filling the space with an unrelated image. The product’s comfort settings explicitly state the contract: real-world visual evidence is always on for new answers, and generated artwork is never used as evidence.

For a reader who wants more than the composed summary, **Evidence & research details** opens a focused deeper-dive view. The full answer expands on one side, still carrying citations in context. On the other side are the visual evidence and a numbered source list. Each source shows its title, publisher, relationship to the answer, and a direct link to inspect it. A research summary explains how the material was approached, while a compact record identifies when the research was performed and which answer style and model were used. This deeper layer is intentionally available without forcing every user to read it before continuing. A casual reader can understand the answer; a skeptical or highly interested reader can inspect its foundations.

## The moment that makes CuriosityPedia different

Under every answer is the question **“Where should curiosity go next?”** Two large, colorful path cards offer two genuinely different follow-up directions. Each card includes a short description of its angle and the full next question. They are not generic suggestions floating outside the answer; they are the two open edges of the current point in the journey.

Choosing either path begins another live research turn. The new answer does not erase the old one. It becomes its child, preserving the relationship between the question that was answered and the question that the answer inspired. The second unused option remains available as an open path, so the user can return later and find out where it leads. This is the key emotional difference from ordinary search: a decision does not make the alternative disappear.

The user is not trapped by the two suggestions. **Pick a path for me** lets CuriosityPedia choose when both are appealing. **Try two different questions** redraws both directions, with controls for making the replacements more practical, more surprising, or simply different. An optional note lets the user say what should change. These controls keep the experience guided without taking away agency.

If the user revisits an older answer and chooses one of its still-open questions, CuriosityPedia explains that a new visible branch will be created and that the existing route will remain intact. History is therefore something the user can build upon, not something they must overwrite.

## Seeing the journey as a tree

At any point, the user can switch from **Stage** to **Journey map**. The Stage answers “What am I learning right now?” The map answers “How did I get here, what else have I explored, and what possibilities are still open?”

The map begins with the journey title and three simple counts: completed turns, open paths, and accumulated sources. Below that is a searchable tree. Completed questions appear as solid cards joined by lines; unanswered possibilities appear as lighter, dotted open-path cards. The active route is visually emphasized, and the current turn carries an unmistakable **You are here** marker. Color helps distinguish states without being the only signal.

The tree can be viewed at different levels of detail, from a compact overview to topic-oriented nodes or fuller cards. Large explorations can be panned and zoomed; a miniature overview helps the user understand where the visible portion sits within the whole. A search box finds both completed turns and unanswered questions. An **Open paths** filter is useful when the user is less interested in reviewing history than in finding the next promising place to continue. A structured outline provides the same journey as a navigable hierarchy for people who prefer lists, keyboard navigation, or a more compact representation.

Selecting a completed turn opens an inspector with its question, a short answer preview, its place in the current route, and actions to open the full answer or focus the map around that branch. Selecting an unanswered path shows exactly where it will grow from and asks for confirmation before starting new research. This is especially important in a mature journey with many branches: the user can explore the map freely without accidentally spending a research turn.

On a phone, the experience becomes vertical. The route grows downward, the current branch remains visually strong, and controls are reorganized into touch-friendly rows. The inspector stays out of the way until it is needed. The product does not try to squeeze a desktop diagram into a tiny viewport; it preserves the sense of ancestry and branching in a form that can be followed by scrolling.

## Returning later: Library and Bookmarks

Every journey is persistent. The **Library** displays saved explorations as editorial cards rather than chat transcripts. Each card shows the journey’s title and topic labels, its performer, and counts for turns, sources, and open questions. The user can search by title, question, or topic; filter by performer; and choose whether hidden journeys should appear. A journey can be resumed, renamed, pinned, hidden, snapshotted, exported, or removed. Removal requires a clear confirmation, protecting a tree that may represent hours of exploration.

**Bookmarks** is more personal and granular. It combines whole journeys with individual questions the user deliberately saved. The page can show everything, only saved questions, or pinned journeys. Search, performer filters, and sorting make it practical as the collection grows. Items are grouped into **Today**, **This week**, and **Earlier**, so the page feels like a memory of recent curiosity rather than an undifferentiated database. A saved question remembers the journey and topic around it, the performer who researched it, the source count, and the exact path back to the answer.

## Limits, preferences, and a sense of control

CuriosityPedia treats usage as part of the user experience rather than a surprise that appears only when something is blocked. The **Usage** page shows how many live research runs are available now, how many were used during the rolling 24-hour window, and when individual slots will return. It separately shows the capacity of the saved-journey library and, in a collapsible detail, provider spending against its limit. The language explains that there is no magical midnight reset: each use leaves the rolling window 24 hours after it occurred. Guest users are reminded that their limits and saved journeys belong to the current browser session; signed-in users are told that the allowance follows their identity.

The **Settings** page is organized around understandable outcomes. The user can change the interface and answer language, choose a default research model, and decide whether answers should be a quick read, balanced, or a deeper dive. Text can be previewed at small, medium, large, or extra-large sizes before saving. Reduced motion is available for comfort and accessibility. The page also shows whether preferences are synchronized to an account or stored only in the guest session.

The interface supports multiple languages, including right-to-left presentation where appropriate, and its important interactions have keyboard and screen-reader semantics. Motion is decorative and orienting rather than essential: reducing it does not remove access to any part of the journey.

## The overall experience

CuriosityPedia is for the moment when someone does not merely want a fact; they want to stay with a subject long enough for it to become interesting. It gives research a beginning, a present location, a visible history, and several possible futures. The product is guided, but not restrictive; playful, but not careless with evidence; visually expressive, but structured enough to support serious reading.

The simplest way to explain it is this: **CuriosityPedia turns a question into a researched choose-your-own-path journey, then keeps the whole map so you can return, branch, and continue learning without losing where your curiosity has already been.**
